What Is Paint Correction?

Paint correction is the process of using machine polishers and cutting compounds to remove surface-level defects from your vehicle's clear coat. Swirl marks, light scratches, oxidation, and water spot etching are all defects that live in the outermost layer of the paint — and machine polishing safely removes them by leveling the clear coat surface.

The result is a smooth, uniform surface that reflects light evenly — producing the deep gloss and clarity your paint had when it was new. It's also the required first step before applying ceramic coating, since coating locks in the paint condition permanently.

Common Issues We Fix

Signs Your Paint Needs Correction

Swirl Marks

Circular patterns visible in direct sunlight — usually caused by improper washing, automatic car washes, or wiping the paint with a dry cloth. One of the most common paint defects.

Light Scratches

Scratches that haven't reached the base coat or primer — these live in the clear coat and can be polished out. If you can't feel the scratch with your fingernail, it's likely correctable.

Water Spots

Mineral deposits left behind when water evaporates on the paint surface. In Charleston's hard water conditions, these can etch into the clear coat over time and require polishing to remove.

Oxidation & Fading

Long-term UV exposure breaks down the clear coat, leaving paint looking dull, chalky, or faded. Machine polishing removes the damaged outer layer and restores the clear coat's clarity.

Buffer Trails

Holograms or trails left by improper use of polishing equipment. Common after low-quality detail shops apply machine polishers incorrectly. Professional correction removes these.

Dull, Flat Appearance

If your car doesn't have the deep gloss it once did — even after washing — the clear coat surface has likely accumulated enough micro-defects to scatter rather than reflect light. Correction restores the depth.

How We Do It

Every paint correction starts with a thorough inspection and follows a proven multi-step process. We use professional-grade Koch-Chemie polishing products on every job.

1

Decontamination Wash

We start with a full decontamination wash including iron fallout removal and clay bar treatment. This ensures all bonded contaminants are off the paint before polishing begins — polishing over contamination creates more defects.

2

Paint Inspection

Under direct lighting, we assess the type and severity of defects across every panel. This determines whether single-stage or multi-stage polishing is needed and what compound and pad combinations to use.

3

Machine Polishing

Using a dual-action or rotary polisher with the appropriate compound and pad, we work panel by panel to level the clear coat and remove defects. Heavy defects may require a cutting stage followed by a finishing stage to maximize gloss.

4

Panel Wipe & Inspection

After polishing each panel, we wipe down with an IPA solution and inspect under direct lighting to confirm defects are removed. Any remaining high spots are re-addressed.

5

Sealant Application

Following correction, a paint sealant is applied to protect the freshly polished surface. If ceramic coating has been requested, it's applied at this stage instead — on a perfectly prepared, defect-free canvas.
